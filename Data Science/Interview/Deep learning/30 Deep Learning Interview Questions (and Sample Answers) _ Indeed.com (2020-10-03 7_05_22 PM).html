30 Deep Learning Interview Questions (and Sample Answers)

April 14, 2020

If you want to work in the field of deep learning, you should prepare for an in-depth interview. Many companies have multiple rounds of interviews that test your technical and programming skills, your ability to come up with solutions to open-ended problems, how well you analyze data with a range of methods, the way you apply deep learning effectively and your mastery of key concepts in machine learning and data science. In this article, we look at some of the most common deep learning interview questions and offer sample answers.

Basic deep learning interview questions

These questions allow an interviewer to evaluate your understanding of the basic concepts of deep learning:

  • What is deep learning?
  • What is a neural network? Explain with an example.
  • How does perception work?
  • What is data normalization and why do we need it?
  • What are hyperparameters? Name a few used in any neural network.
  • What is Convolutional Neural Network?
  • What is the Boltzmann machine?
  • What is RNN?
  • What is a dropout?
  • What are tensors?

Deep learning interview questions about experience and background

These questions allow an interviewer to evaluate your qualifications for the position:

  • What have you been working on for the past few years?
  • What deep learning platforms are you familiar with, and how proficient are you with them?
  • What kind of deep learning problems have you addressed, and how did you solve them?
  • What is the most interesting project you have ever worked on?
  • What programming languages do you know?
  • What's your research experience in deep learning?
  • Explain the day-to-day work of a deep learning engineer.
  • Which programming language or technologies should someone have or learn to be a deep learning engineer?
  • What are some common problems faced by deep learning engineers?
  • What subjects would you include in a one-day deep learning crash course and why?

In-depth deep learning interview questions

These questions help an interviewer assess the extent of your knowledge in deep learning:

  • When it comes to training an artificial neural network, what could be the reason why the loss doesn't decrease in a few epochs?
  • Why is weight initialization important in a neural network?
  • Which is better, shallow networks or deep ones? Why?
  • Why is zero initialization not recommended for weight initialization?
  • Can you explain what vanishing gradient is? Why is this harmful?
  • Do you think deep learning performs better than machine learning? If so, why?
  • Can you give some real-life examples where auto-encoders can be applied?
  • What are supervised and unsupervised learning algorithms in deep learning?
  • How do you deploy a deep learning model?
  • Do we need feature extraction in deep learning?

Sample deep learning interview questions and answers

Here's a look at some of the common deep learning interview questions, as well as tips on how to answer them with sample answers:

  1. What AI and deep learning tools are you familiar with, and how proficient are you in them?
  2. Can you explain what a neural network is?
  3. How is deep learning used in practice?
  4. What kinds of deep learning problems have you encountered, and how did you handle them?
  5. Have you earned any deep learning certifications?
  6. Which do you think is more important: model performance or model accuracy?
  7. What are the ethical implications of using deep learning?
  8. How does deep learning differ from other machine learning algorithms?
  9. What is the most interesting project you have ever worked on?
  10. Can you give some real-life examples where auto-encoders can be applied?

1. What AI and deep learning tools are you familiar with, and how proficient are you in them?

This question allows you to elaborate on your skills and gives the interviewer a chance to assess your confidence level with AI and deep learning tools. To answer this question effectively, state the tools you have used in your previous job and describe how they work.

Example: "I'm familiar with TensorFlow and Neural Designer. I have used both in my previous job for the past five years. TensorFlow comes with a flexible scheme of tools, resources and libraries that allow me to build and deploy machine learning applications. Neural Designer, on the other hand, is good at discovering complex relationships, predicting actual trends from data sets and recognizing unknown patterns by means of neural networks."

2. Can you explain what a neural network is?

If you are a deep learning engineer, you need to have a thorough understanding not only of coding but also of each of the components that go into creating a successful deep learning algorithm. To answer this question effectively, explain with an example.

Example: "**The primary function of a neural network is to receive a set of inputs, perform complex calculations and then use the output to solve the problem. A neural network is used for a range of applications. One example is classification; there are many classifiers available today, such as random forest, decision trees, support vector, logistic regression and so on, and of course neural networks."

3. How is deep learning used in practice?

Interviewers usually asked this question to determine how much "hands-on" experience you have in deep learning. This question tests your technical knowledge. To answer this question effectively, provide several examples of how deep learning is used in practice.

Example: "Deep learning is used across all industries for a variety of tasks. Commercial applications that use face recognition, medical research tools that aim to reuse drugs for new diseases and open-source platforms that come with consumer recommendation apps are some of the examples of deep learning applications."

4. What kinds of deep learning problems have you encountered, and how did you handle them?

Hiring managers usually ask this question to assess your critical-thinking and problem-solving abilities. To answer this question effectively, walk the hiring manager through how the problem came about and what actions you took to resolve it. Make sure to choose a problem that is related to the job you're applying for.

Example: "Some of the deep learning problems I have encountered include overfitting and underfitting. Overfitting occurs when the model learns the noise and details in the training data to the degree that it adversely affects the execution of the model on new information. It usually occurs with non-linear models that have more flexibility when learning a target function. For example, if a model is looking at trucks and cars, but only recognizes trucks that have a specific box shape, it may not be able to recognize a truck trailer because there's only one particular type of truck it learns in training. The model works well on training data, but not in the real world.

Underfitting, on the other hand, alludes to a model that can neither generalize to new information or is well-trained on data. This usually occurs when there is incorrect and fewer data to train a model. Underfitting has both poor accuracy and performance. To avoid underfitting and overfitting, I have re-sampled the data to estimate the model accuracy and to evaluate the model."

5. Have you earned any deep learning certifications?

Being able to demonstrate that you have successfully completed a professional certificate program in deep learning can be a huge advantage, as this lets the hiring manager know that you are professionally trained and will not require additional mentoring or instruction in order to develop deep learning algorithms. You should answer this question honestly and do more than just describe your certifications.

Example: "In order to fully expand my knowledge about deep learning, I recently earned my deep learning professional certificate. The knowledge I acquired has enabled me to exponentially streamline my previous employer's customer service."

6. Which do you think is more important: model performance or model accuracy?

Interviewers asked this question to evaluate your skill in designing deep learning models. Many candidates justify the need for both performance and accuracy, but when it comes to deep learning, accuracy matters more than performance, as inaccurate information is not useful.

Example: *"**While both performance and accuracy are important and subjective to the specific application you are developing, accuracy is usually more important. If your deep learning algorithm generates inaccurate information, it doesn't matter how fast it does it."*

7. What are the ethical implications of using deep learning?

This question allows the hiring manager to explore your knowledge of the ethical implications of deep learning technologies. To answer this question effectively, you should be able to discuss the problems associated with data privacy; segmentation based on sensitive variables such as age, gender or race; models based on biased data inputs; methods to verify and correct models that have caused actual bias.

Example: *"**Ethics has to be seen as an important practical consideration when using or developing deep learning systems. If we fail to do so, the consequences could be serious. The last few years have been packed with stories of AI not only showing theoretical bias but also causing discrimination in the real world."*

8. How does deep learning differ from other machine learning algorithms?

The purpose of this question is to assess your ability to explain deep learning concepts in a simple yet comprehensive manner. To answer this question effectively, be as detailed and specific as possible.

Example: "Deep learning, also called "deep neural network" or "deep neural learning", is a form of artificial intelligence (AI) function that works like a human brain in processing data and generating patterns for use in decision making. It is a subfield of machine learning in AI that comes with networks capable of learning unsupervised from unlabeled or unstructured data."

9. What is the most interesting project you have ever worked on?

This is one of the most common competency-based questions for any role that requires project management experience. Interviewers usually ask this question to see how well you can manage a situation or a project, what your approach is to resolving challenges and how your abilities would help you to successfully lead a project. They also want to gain insight into how you handle stress and know what your work ethic is like.

To answer this question effectively, use the STAR (Situation, Task, Action, Result) interview response technique to make a concise (yet in-depth) answer. Make sure your response demonstrates your abilities with hitting deadlines, making decisions, setting priorities or delegating tasks.

Example: "One of the most interesting projects I have ever worked on was the 12 Sigma project, which leverages the power of deep learning to train an artificial intelligence algorithm that would help physicians examine CT scan images more quickly and efficiently. I found it so interesting because of the impact it had on hospitals. Hospitals using the model can get results in under 15 minutes, saving at least four to five hours of physician's work. I also enjoyed working with the rest of the team members. I think it was such a pleasure being able to see a project from start to finish. I really learned so much about communication, teamwork and the importance of having and sticking to a plan."

Related: How to Use the STAR Interview Response Technique

10. Can you give some real-life examples where auto-encoders can be applied?

The purpose of this question is to evaluate your technical knowledge. To answer this question effectively, make sure that each example you provide includes a brief explanation.

Example: "*Some real-life examples where auto-encoders can be applied include* image coloring, which is when an auto-encoder is used for transforming any black and white picture into a colored image, and feature variation, which is when it uses only the required feature of an image and creates the output by eliminating any noise or unnecessary interruption. Another example is denoising image, which occurs when denoising auto-encoders try to eliminate the identity-function risk by randomly corrupting input, which the auto-encoder must then denoise or reconstruct."
